Silent Gear adds a suite of machines and special blocks that sit between raw material gathering and finished gear. Some are simple crafting stations you build early; others are multi-block structures you work toward as you progress through the mod's material tiers.

## Crafting stations

### Gear Smithing Table

The Gear Smithing Table is the primary workstation for crafting Silent Gear items. Place it and right-click to open the crafting interface, then insert a blueprint (or template) along with the required parts to assemble your gear. Almost every Silent Gear tool, weapon, or armor piece is made here.

### Stone Anvil

The Stone Anvil is an early-game block for crafting gear parts without the need for blueprints. It works similarly to the vanilla crafting table but uses shaped recipes specific to Silent Gear parts. Craft it at the start of a new world before you have access to more advanced materials.

## Processing machines

### Material Grader

The Material Grader assigns a quality grade (E through SS) to raw materials. Higher grades boost the material's stat contributions when it is used in a gear part. The grader uses a catalyst item to determine the grade distribution — better catalysts shift results toward higher grades.

### Salvager

The Salvager disassembles gear and parts back into their component materials. Insert a piece of gear and it will return the parts (and optionally break those parts down further into raw materials). How much you recover depends on the gear's remaining durability and the configured loss rates.

[Learn more about the Salvager](/machines/salvager)

### Metal Press

The Metal Press compresses materials into plating. Plating items are used as upgrade parts on gear to add protection or other properties. Place a material and the appropriate die in the press to produce the corresponding plate.

## Alloy makers

Alloy makers combine multiple simple materials into a single compound material with blended stats. There are five alloy makers, each specializing in a different category of materials.

| Machine | Material category | Input slots |
|---|---|---|
| Crude Mixer | Any (no category filter) | 4 |
| Alloy Forge | Metals, dusts | 6 |
| Recrystallizer | Gems, dusts | 6 |
| Refabricator | Cloth, fiber, slime | 6 |
| Super Mixer | Any (no category filter) | 8 |

[Learn more about alloy makers](/machines/alloy-makers)

## Special structures

### Starlight Charger

The Starlight Charger is a pedestal-style block that gathers energy from starlight at night and uses that energy to apply the *starcharged* modifier to materials. A starcharged material improves the special properties of gear crafted from it. The charger requires a multi-block structure of pillar blocks at its corners to determine the maximum charge tier it can apply.

[Learn more about the Starlight Charger](/machines/starlight-charger)

## Ores and natural materials

Silent Gear adds several ores and natural resources used throughout its crafting systems:

- **Bort ore** — found in stone and deepslate layers; drops bort gemstone, used in early compound gem recipes
- **Crimson Iron ore** — found in the Nether (stone and blackstone variants); smelts into crimson iron ingots
- **Azure Silver ore** — found in the Overworld; smelts into azure silver ingots, the foundation of the starcharged material ecosystem
- **Netherwood** — a Nether-native wood with full block set (logs, planks, slabs, stairs, fence, door, leaves, sapling); fire-resistant and used in some Silent Gear crafting recipes

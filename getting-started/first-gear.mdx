Silent Gear tools are not crafted with vanilla recipes. Instead, you first make individual **parts** from raw materials using blueprints, then assemble those parts together with a gear blueprint. This system lets you choose exactly what every component is made from, giving you full control over the tool's stats and special abilities (traits).

## Before you begin

You need:

- Silent Gear installed and a new world loaded (see [Installation](/getting-started/installation))
- Basic materials: sticks, stone, wood planks, or any other material you want to try
- JEI or EMI installed (optional but strongly recommended for browsing recipes in-game)

## Understanding gear parts

Every gear item is made up of several **part slots**. The most important ones for a basic tool are:

| Part | Role |
|---|---|
| **Main** | Determines the gear type (pickaxe head, sword blade, etc.) and contributes the most to stats |
| **Rod** | The handle; contributes durability and some stats |
| **Tip** | Optional upgrade that modifies the tool tip's stats |
| **Binding** | Optional part that can add traits or stats |
| **Grip** | Optional handle upgrade |

<Note>
The **main part** determines what kind of gear you are assembling. A pickaxe main part produces a pickaxe; a sword main part produces a sword. You must choose the correct gear blueprint to match the main part you crafted.
</Note>

## Step-by-step: crafting your first tool

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get your starter blueprints">
    When you join a new world, you will automatically receive a **Blueprint Package** in your inventory. Right-click it to open it and receive a set of starter blueprints. These include blueprints for common part types such as the rod, tip, and several gear types.

    If you did not receive a blueprint package, or you need additional blueprints, craft them manually:

    ```
    Blueprint Paper (crafted from paper) + the item whose blueprint you want
    ```

    Blueprint paper can also be crafted into a **Blueprint Book** to keep all your blueprints organised in a single item slot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Consult the Material Book">
    Craft a **Material Book** (`material_book`) to look up every material that Silent Gear knows about. Right-click the Material Book to browse materials, their stats (harvest level, durability, speed, etc.), and their traits. Use this to decide which materials you want to use for each part.

    <Tip>
    Early game, stone and wood are reliable starting materials. Iron parts are a significant upgrade once you have smelted some iron ingots.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Craft the rod part">
    Place a **Rod Blueprint** and your chosen material (for example, two sticks or two iron ingots arranged as you would for a vanilla tool handle) in the crafting grid. The blueprint remains in your inventory after crafting and can be reused indefinitely.

    The resulting **Rod** item shows the material's name in its tooltip. Hover over it to see the stats it will contribute.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Craft the main part">
    Use the appropriate **gear blueprint** (for example, a Pickaxe Blueprint) together with your chosen head material in the crafting grid. The shape mirrors the vanilla pickaxe head pattern but uses the blueprint instead of a tool item.

    The resulting main part item (for example, **Pickaxe Head**) determines the gear type you will assemble in the next step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Craft optional parts (tip, binding, grip)">
    Optional parts add extra stats or traits on top of what the main and rod provide. Use the corresponding blueprint (Tip Blueprint, Binding Blueprint, etc.) with a material in the crafting grid to create them.

    <Note>
    You do not need optional parts to assemble gear. Start with just a main part and a rod if you want to keep things simple.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assemble the gear">
    Place the **gear blueprint** (for example, the Pickaxe Blueprint) along with your main part, rod, and any optional parts together in a crafting grid — no specific arrangement is required, just place them all in the grid. The output slot will show your finished tool.

    Blueprints are **not** consumed when assembling gear — they are returned to your inventory. Only single-use **templates** are consumed on use. Blueprints can be used unlimited times.

    <Warning>
    If the output slot is empty, check that your main part matches the gear blueprint type. A Sword Head will not combine with a Pickaxe Blueprint.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the gear tooltip">
    Hover over your finished tool to see its full stat breakdown in the tooltip. Stats shown include:

    - **Durability** — total uses before the tool breaks
    - **Harvest Speed** — how fast it mines the relevant blocks
    - **Harvest Tier** — the material level (stone, iron, diamond, etc.)
    - **Attack Damage** and **Attack Speed** (for weapons)
    - **Traits** — special abilities contributed by the materials used, listed at the bottom of the tooltip

    Hold **Shift** for an extended tooltip that shows the contribution of each individual part.
  </Step>
</Steps>
